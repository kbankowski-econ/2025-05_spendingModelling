## ✅ Done since 2026-06-26

**Fiscal multipliers reworked**
- Switched `tab:multipliers` to **present-value cumulative multipliers**: output and
  own-spending are discounted by the model's β before cumulating
  (`makeMultipliers.py`). The exact formula
  $\big(\sum \beta^{t}\Delta Y_t\big)/\big(\sum \beta^{t}\Delta G_t\big)$ is now in
  the table note.
- Extended the computation to long horizons and **consolidated the outer years
  (50–250y) into a single "Long-term" column** (the converged value); the note
  explains it. Columns are now 1y / 5y / 10y / 20y / 25y / Long-term.
- Added a **permanent-shock comparison table in new Appendix D**
  (`tab:multipliersPerm`), fed by seven new `Model_HumanCapital_exp_*_perm`
  models. Diagnosis: the AR(1) (transitory) cumulative multiplier rises with the
  horizon because the spending denominator saturates while durable HC/R&D output
  keeps accruing; a permanent shock's denominator grows too, so its multiplier
  converges to the long-run multiplier and is smaller at intermediate horizons.
- Confirmed the discount factor is reasonable: β=0.9985 with trend growth implies a
  steady-state real rate of ≈2.2% (AE) / ≈3.7% (EMDE); the gentle effect on the
  multiplier is because output and spending co-grow (the wedge in the ratio is
  r−g ≈ 0.6%).

**Prose updated to match the numbers**
- Abstract and intro multiplier figures replaced with current values (public
  investment ≈1.8–3.3 in AEs, ≈4.5–6.5 in EMDEs at 25y; R&D ≈4.9 AE; all rise
  further long-run).
- §4.2 literature claim **softened** to "broadly in line at medium horizons, larger
  in the long run."

**Table formatting**
- Fixed the source→note spacing and left-alignment in the three note-bearing tables
  via a new `\tabnote` macro (source and note share one left-aligned minipage with a
  fixed 2 mm gap). Figures keep `\source`/`\desc`; Tables 1 (source only) and 4
  (notation, no note) verified consistent.
