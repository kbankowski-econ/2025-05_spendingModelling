## ✅ Done since 2026-06-23

**Calibration — Frisch elasticity set to Galí's value**
- `phi` (inverse Frisch) changed **1.2 → 5** (Frisch 0.2), following Galí (2015, Ch. 3),
  in both the full model (`parameters_common.macro`) and the NK benchmark
  (`modelTemplateNK.mod`). Documented in §3 with a new Galí (2015) citation. All models re-run.

**Standard shocks now AR(1)**
- The seven standard expansion sims feeding the transmission figure (Fig 1) and the
  multiplier table changed from **permanent** to **AR(1), persistence 0.9** (1%-of-GDP
  impact), AE and EMDE alike. `fig:standardShocks` and `tab:multipliers` regenerated;
  figure/table prose and the `makeMultipliers` docstring updated.

**Government-consumption transmission — investigated, then settled**
- Built a from-scratch canonical New Keynesian benchmark and **verified numerically that it
  equals Galí (2015, Ch. 3) plus a wasteful government block** to machine precision
  (max |diff| ≈ 1.3e-5). The cross-check is kept in `investigations/galiCheck/`.
- Established that the sign of the gov-consumption response is governed jointly by the Frisch
  elasticity and shock persistence. Under the adopted standard Frisch + realistic AR(1)
  persistence the shock is **inflationary** (as expected); the earlier "deflation" was a
  feature of a more elastic labor supply and permanent shocks.
- **Appendix C reframed** to a clean robustness section, *Government Consumption: Robustness
  to Model Structure*: the model-simplification ladder (`fig:simplifiedGc`) shows the
  inflationary sign is a property of the New Keynesian structure, not of the growth/public-
  capital channels. The earlier duration and persistence figures (Figs 9/10) and their seven
  NK variant models were removed.

**Reproducibility & housekeeping**
- All four paper tables now generated by `invoke` tasks (`makeMultipliers`,
  `makeParametersTable`, `makeEfficiencyGapsTable`, `makeNotationTable`); `run-all`
  regenerates every figure, contribution panel, and `\input` table — no hand-run generators.
- `runModel.m`'s model list annotated with each simulation's role in the paper (figure /
  table / section), in labelled sections (standard expansions · policy experiments · Jamaica ·
  deflation benchmarks).
